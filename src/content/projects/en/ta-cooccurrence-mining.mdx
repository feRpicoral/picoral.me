## What it is

A class project that asks a single question: do technical-analysis (TA) signals fire together across different US equities at frequencies above chance, and if so, which ones — conditioned on intraday session and sector?

The data is one year of 5-minute OHLCV bars from [Stooq](https://stooq.com/db/h/) for the US ticker universe. Each timestamp becomes a transaction:

```
X_t = { TICKER:EVENT | event fires for ticker at time t }
```

with 13 binary event signals derived from TA-Lib indicators — moving-average crosses, MACD crosses, RSI oversold/overbought, Bollinger Band breaks and reverts, VWAP crosses, volume spikes. Then FP-Growth and H-Mine over the transaction database discover frequent itemsets and association rules with the usual support/confidence/lift triple.

## How it works

### Pipeline

Ten Jupyter notebooks, runnable in sequence:

- `01_preprocessing` — load raw Stooq ASCII bars, normalize CEST → ET, filter to regular trading hours.
- `02_feature_engineering` — calculate TA indicators via TA-Lib and generate the 13 binary signals.
- `03_technical_analysis` — visualize and validate signal generation.
- `04_event_encoding` — transform signals into `TICKER:EVENT` transactions; add market-cap weighting.
- `05_global_mining` — FP-Growth on the full dataset; frequent itemsets and association rules.
- `06_session_mining` — separate mining for the Open (9:30–10:30 ET), Midday (10:30–15:00 ET), and Close (15:00–16:00 ET) sessions; compare patterns.
- `07_temporal_leadlag` — temporal stability across monthly folds; identify persistent patterns.
- `08_sectoral_mining` — intra- vs. inter-sector patterns; flow networks via NetworkX.
- `09_baseline_validation` — raw-price baseline and timestamp-shuffle placebo to filter spurious rules.

### Cross-ticker filtering

The interesting rules are inter-asset: `{AAPL:RSI_LONG, NVDA:RSI_LONG} => {MSFT:RSI_LONG}`, not intra-ticker reformulations of textbook TA. The mining stage filters for rules whose antecedent and consequent involve different tickers.

### Market-cap weighting

Transactions are weighted by participating-ticker market cap so the headline patterns are economically significant, not driven by penny stocks. The raw, unweighted view is preserved alongside for comparison.

### Validation

Two safeguards against false-positive rules:

- **Timestamp shuffle placebo** — re-mine the same data with timestamps shuffled across tickers. Any rule that survives the shuffle is spurious by definition.
- **Temporal folds** — mine across monthly folds and require the rule to persist out of sample before it's reported.
